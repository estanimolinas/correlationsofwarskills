# Template 02 — Research question

**Lamont chapter (topic):** Research questions and puzzles
**Use when:** after Template 01 (epistemology) and before any data work
**Output:** one well-formed research question, the puzzle behind it, and a feasibility check

A weak research question is the single most common cause of stalled IR projects. Lamont's framing — *puzzle first, question second* — forces you to articulate what is surprising before you write the question that resolves it.

## Section 3: Question type

- [ ] **Descriptive** — characterising a phenomenon (*how widespread? how varied? how has it changed?*)
- [ ] **Explanatory / causal** — accounting for variation in an outcome
- [ ] **Interpretive** — reconstructing the meaning, framing, or constitutive logic of a practice
- [ ] **Normative** — assessing a phenomenon against a stated standard (*should states do X? on what grounds?*)
