# Template 07 — Research ethics checklist

**Lamont chapter (topic):** Research ethics
**Use when:** before any data collection involving human subjects (interviews, surveys, ethnography, focus groups, sensitive archives) — and as a precaution for purely secondary-data projects, where minimal-risk certification is still typically required by IRB / REC
**Output:** a completed ethics checklist plus an IRB / REC protocol stub if human-subjects research is involved

Ethics is not paperwork. Most IR field research involves asymmetries of power, and a number of recent cases — anthropologists, sociologists, and IR scholars working in conflict zones — show what happens when ethics is treated as a formality.
