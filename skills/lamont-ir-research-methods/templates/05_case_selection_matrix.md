# Template 05 — Case selection matrix

**Lamont chapter (topic):** Case study methods
**Use when:** your research design (Template 04) calls for case-study or small-N comparative methods
**Output:** a justified set of cases, with selection logic stated explicitly and rejected alternatives documented

Reviewers reject more case-study papers for *unjustified case selection* than for any other single methodological reason. This template forces you to state the logic, name the cases, and show your work.

---

## Section 1: Case-selection logic

Tick the logic(s) that apply. More than one can apply (e.g., a most-similar pair drawn from a typical-case population).

- [ ] **Typical case** — selected because it exemplifies a broader population (good for theory-confirming or mechanism-tracing studies)
- [ ] **Deviant case** — anomalous with respect to existing theory (good for theory-building or theory-revising studies)
- [ ] **Most-similar systems design** — cases match on most variables but differ on the outcome (isolates the candidate cause)
- [ ] **Most-different systems design** — cases differ on most variables but share the outcome (rules out particularistic explanations)
- [ ] **Mill's method of agreement** — cases share the outcome; look for the shared antecedent
- [ ] **Mill's method of difference** — cases differ on the outcome; look for the difference in antecedents
- [ ] **Extreme case** — selected because it shows the phenomenon at its sharpest
- [ ] **Crucial case** — least-likely or most-likely test for a theory
- [ ] **Process-tracing single case** — selected because within-case mechanism evidence is the goal, not cross-case comparison
- [ ] **Other / pragmatic** — [fill in: state the rationale; "data is available" alone is not sufficient]
