# Template 08 — Write-up outline

**Lamont chapter (topic):** Writing up research
**Use when:** before drafting the article, thesis chapter, or report
**Output:** a section-by-section outline with word budget, figure / table plan, and contribution statement

Outlining before drafting prevents the most common writing-up failure: an introduction that does not match what the paper actually delivers. Fill this in once, then revise as the paper develops.

## Section 2: Contribution statement

In exactly three sentences:

1. What is the puzzle / gap? [fill in: from Templates 02 and 03 §4]
2. What does this paper show / argue? [fill in]
3. Why does it matter for IR? [fill in: theoretical, empirical, or policy payoff]

This three-sentence block becomes the spine of the abstract, the introduction, and the conclusion. If you cannot write it tightly, the contribution is not yet sharp enough.

## Section 6: Pre-submission checks

- [ ] Title is descriptive and contains the contribution (not just the topic)
- [ ] Abstract opens with the puzzle / gap and ends with the contribution
- [ ] Every figure and table is referenced in the text and adds something prose cannot
- [ ] Methods section names the data sources with versions and citations
- [ ] Ethics statement (if applicable) is present
- [ ] Replication statement is present, even if access is restricted
- [ ] References use the journal's style consistently
- [ ] Self-citations are anonymised if the venue requires double-blind review
- [ ] At least one peer or supervisor has read the full draft before submission
